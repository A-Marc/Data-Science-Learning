Probability Practice Set 1
Questions and Solutions

1. You roll a fair six-sided die. What is the probability of getting an even number?

Answer: 1/2 or 0.5 or 50%

Explanation:
Sample space = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6} (total 6 outcomes)
Even numbers = {2, 4, 6} (3 favorable outcomes)
Probability = Number of favorable outcomes / Total outcomes = 3/6 = 1/2


2. Two coins are tossed. Find the sample space associated with this random experiment.

Answer: {HH, HT, TH, TT}

Explanation:
When tossing two coins, each coin can show either Heads (H) or Tails (T).
- First coin H, Second coin H → HH
- First coin H, Second coin T → HT
- First coin T, Second coin H → TH
- First coin T, Second coin T → TT
Total 4 possible outcomes in the sample space.


3. Two six-sided dice are rolled. What is the probability that the sum is 7?

Answer: 1/6 or approximately 0.167 or 16.67%

Explanation:
Total possible outcomes = 6 × 6 = 36
Combinations that sum to 7:
(1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1) = 6 favorable outcomes
Probability = 6/36 = 1/6


4. A bag contains 3 red, 2 blue, and 5 green balls. What is the probability of randomly selecting a red ball?

Answer: 3/10 or 0.3 or 30%

Explanation:
Total balls = 3 + 2 + 5 = 10
Red balls = 3
Probability = Number of red balls / Total balls = 3/10


5. A card is drawn at random from a deck. Find the probability of:

a) Getting a heart
Answer: 1/4 or 0.25 or 25%

Explanation:
Standard deck has 52 cards with 4 suits (hearts, diamonds, clubs, spades).
Hearts = 13 cards
Probability = 13/52 = 1/4

b) Getting a face card
Answer: 3/13 or approximately 0.231 or 23.1%

Explanation:
Face cards are Jack, Queen, and King.
Each suit has 3 face cards, and there are 4 suits.
Total face cards = 3 × 4 = 12
Probability = 12/52 = 3/13
